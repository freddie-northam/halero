# F1

The F1 module turns Halero into a customizable Formula 1 dashboard, driven
by the [OpenF1 API](https://openf1.org). It has three layers, all reached
from the **F1** entry in the sidebar:

- **Season companion**: the calendar of race weekends, each weekend's
  session times in your timezone (FP1/FP2/FP3, Qualifying, Sprint, Race),
  results, and driver + constructor standings.
- **Race explorer**: deep-dive any session with tyre strategy, lap times,
  position changes, pit stops, the race-control/flag timeline, team radio,
  overtakes, weather, the derived starting grid, and head-to-head driver
  comparisons.
- **Live timing**: a live timing tower (running order, gaps, tyres,
  auto-refreshing), live weather, and a race track's current session.

Everything is arranged on **boards**: named, customizable grids of widgets
you add, reorder, resize, and configure. A fresh install ships a **Season**
board and a **Live** board; add your own from the F1 page.

## Connecting

Open **Settings > Integrations** and connect **F1**. Historical data (2023
onward) is free and needs no account, so connecting is one click and the
schedule, results, and standings start syncing.

F1 sessions also flow into the rest of Halero: every session becomes an
entity, so races show up in universal search and on Today and the Calendar.

## Live timing (your own OpenF1 account)

Real-time timing during a live session requires an OpenF1 paid
subscription. Halero ships no shared credentials: you bring your own. On
the **Live** board, use the **Live Timing** control to connect with your
OpenF1 account username and password. Halero stores them encrypted (the
same AES-256-GCM path as every other connection credential), exchanges them
for a short-lived token as needed, and never sends them anywhere but
OpenF1. Disconnect at any time; the free tier keeps working without it.

Whether a session is live is free information, so the Live board tells you
when nothing is running even before you add an account.

## How the data is stored

- **Sessions** sync durably into the entity spine, so they are searchable
  and appear on your timeline.
- **Race detail** (laps, stints, pit stops, positions, race control, team
  radio, overtakes, weather) is fetched the first time you open a session
  and then cached. Historical F1 data never changes once a session ends, so
  this cache doubles as a durable local archive.
- **Live timing** is never stored: it is proxied straight through while a
  session is running.

OpenF1 limits requests to 3 per second; Halero throttles its fetches to
stay under that.
